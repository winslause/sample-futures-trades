About Bitcoin

Bitcoin (BTC) is the first decentralized cryptocurrency, created in 2009 by an anonymous entity known as Satoshi Nakamoto. It operates on a peer-to-peer network without central authority or banks.

Bitcoin is often referred to as "digital gold" due to its store of value properties. It has a fixed supply of 21 million coins, with new coins created through the mining process approximately every 10 minutes.

Key Features:

  • Decentralized: No central authority controls the network
  • Limited Supply: Only 21 million BTC will ever exist
  • Secure: Protected by cryptographic proof-of-work
  • Borderless: Can be sent anywhere in the world
  • Transparent: All transactions are publicly verifiable
